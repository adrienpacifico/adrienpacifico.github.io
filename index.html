About Me:

Economics:
I am a public economist and my research has mainly focused on the taxation of households. I am using the microsimulation software OpenFisca to conduct my analysis. I consider myself as literate in the French tax-benefit system, and also in French administrative databases that concern households. I also have a vivid interest towards equality of opportunity issues, determinants of fertility, and normative taxation with respect to time. Please browse the "Research" section to know more about my past, present, and future research.
Python and Data science:
During my PhD, I invested heavily in modern data science tools that are becoming new standards in the industry. I conduct all my analysis with Python, and especially use the Pandas and the Statsmodels packages. It has allow me to work efficiently on medium size complex administrative databases (up to 20 Go, with complex data-structure handling when dealing with different entities such as individuals, households, tax-households, families and so on). Moreover, I think that the usage of Open-source software is critical for research reproducibility in economics.
