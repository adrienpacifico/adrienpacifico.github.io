About Me:

Economics:
I am a public economist and my research focuses on households taxation. For this purpose, I use the microsimulation software OpenFisca. This allowed me to develop an expert knowledge of the French tax-benefit system, as well as French administrative household databases. I also have a vivid interest towards equality of opportunity issues, determinants of fertility, and the timing of taxation. Please browse the "Research" section to know more about my past, present, and future research.
Python and Data Science:
During my PhD, I invested heavily in modern data science tools which are becoming common standards in the industry. I conduct all my analysis using Python, especially Pandas and the Statsmodels packages. This allows me to work efficiently on medium size (up to 20 Go) complex administrative databases.
Moreover, I think that the usage of Open-source software is critical for research reproducibility in economics.
